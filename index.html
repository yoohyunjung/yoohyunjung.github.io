About me

Hi, I'm Yoohyun, a multiplatform digital journalist. I currently work as a criminal justice reporter in Honolulu, Hawai'i. Prior to that, I was a radio writer for KBS WORLD in Seoul and a data reporter at the Arizona Daily Star, where I started as a metro and public safety reporter in 2014. I was also an education reporter in Arizona for more than two years. I graduated from the University of Arizona School of Journalism. I was also an investigative fellow for Reveal from the Center for Investigative Reporting. Read about my fellowship here.

I like to crunch data, read case files no one else wants to read and tell compelling stories. But I also like being out in the world with people.

Nearly all of my adult life was spent in the Sonoran Desert. In my youth, I roamed the streets of Seoul, where I dumpster-dove every once in a while, got kicked out of too many relatives’ homes, flipped burgers, made uninspired coffee, dreamed of being a Broadway star then later, a punk rock queen, taught English to misbehaving children and hopped on too many trains. Even before that, I lived in Vancouver, B.C. During that time, I did exactly the opposite of what I was told to do.

I don’t remember the exact point in time that I decided that I was going to be a journalist. What I do know is that more than half of what I know about journalism came from a friend who is no longer here. He said to me, “Keep your eyes on the road ahead, kid. But never forget where you’ve been. Be bold. Be brave. Be humble and be yourself.” I kind of live by that.

And I think the road ahead involves me becoming a newsroom innovator, competent data analyst and developer, and effective storyteller. I’m working on getting there.

Check out my LinkedIn profile. Side services you can get from me include: English-Korean translation, English-Japanese translation, freelance reporting in the Tucson area, data analysis and GIS work.
